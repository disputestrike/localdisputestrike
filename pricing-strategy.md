# DisputeStrike Pricing Strategy (Conversion-Optimized)

## Core Principle: Free Tier Should Be Frustrating

The free tier exists to:
1. **Prove value** - Show users the AI works
2. **Create urgency** - Make them hit limits immediately
3. **Drive upgrades** - Make paid tier feel essential

## Psychological Pricing Tactics

### 1. Anchor High, Discount Later
- Show Premium ($99.99) first
- Make Pro ($59.99) look like a "deal"
- Free tier looks like a "trial"

### 2. Scarcity & Urgency
- "Limited time: Get Pro for $49.99/month (save $10)"
- "Only 47 spots left at this price"
- "Price increases to $69.99 on Feb 1st"

### 3. Social Proof
- "Join 2,847 users improving their credit"
- "Average user removes 12 negative accounts"
- "4.8‚òÖ rating from 1,200+ reviews"

### 4. Loss Aversion
- "You're missing out on 24 accounts that could be disputed"
- "Each month you wait costs you points"
- "Don't let errors damage your credit another day"

### 5. Immediate Value
- Show total negative accounts found
- Calculate potential score increase
- Show dollar value of better credit (lower interest rates)

## Free Tier Friction Points (Drive Upgrades)
