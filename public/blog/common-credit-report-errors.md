Here are the five most common credit report errors you need to watch out for:

## 1. Identity Errors
This is the most common type of error. It happens when information belonging to someone with a similar name or Social Security number is mixed into your file. This is often called a "merged file."

## 2. Incorrect Account Status
Sometimes, an account you closed is still reported as open, or a debt you've already paid off is still showing a balance. Even worse, an account might be incorrectly marked as "Charge-off" or "In Collections" when it was actually paid on time.

## 3. Data Management Errors
These are technical glitches where the same debt is listed multiple times (double reporting) or where an account that was removed after a dispute suddenly reappears (reinsertion).

## 4. Outdated Information
Negative information, such as late payments or collections, generally must be removed after **seven years**. Bankruptcies must be removed after ten. If you see old "zombie" debts on your report, they are violating the law.

## 5. Impossible Timelines
This is a favorite for DisputeStrike's AI. We often find accounts where the "Date of Last Activity" is reported as occurring *before* the account was even opened. This is a clear sign of inaccurate data reporting.
