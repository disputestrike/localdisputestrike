# The Hidden Law: How FCRA § 1681i Can Force Bureaus to Delete Negatives

In the world of credit repair, knowledge is power. Most consumers believe that if a negative item is on their credit report, it’s there to stay for seven years. However, federal law provides a powerful "shield" that most people never use: **The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) § 1681i.**

## What is FCRA § 1681i?

Section 1681i of the FCRA is the cornerstone of consumer protection regarding credit reporting. It dictates exactly how credit bureaus (Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax) must handle disputes. 

The most critical part of this law is the **30-day investigation rule**.

> "If the completeness or accuracy of any item of information contained in a consumer's file is disputed by the consumer... the agency shall, free of charge, conduct a reasonable reinvestigation to determine whether the disputed information is inaccurate."

## Why This Matters for You

The law doesn't just say they have to *look* at your dispute; it says they must **verify** it. If they cannot verify the information within 30 days (or 45 days in some specific cases), the law is very clear:

**The information MUST be deleted.**
